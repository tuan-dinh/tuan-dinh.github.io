I am a Ph.D. student in computer science at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, with minor in statistics.
I'm fortunate to be advised by with Prof. Kangwook Lee, with the focus on efficient and robust deep learning.
Previously, I have worked with Prof. Vikas Singh in learning generative models for graph-structured data and medical images. Prior to graduate studies, I have worked with Prof. Tru Cao in building AI systems for disease forecast and healthcare.

I am on the job market for the full-time research positions.

Research

I'm currently interested in two questions: (1) how to make the adaptation of large pretrained models efficient and effective, and (2) how to use machine learning to help address medical problems.
I believe that our research and science will be more meaningful if they can bring more values to the society.
